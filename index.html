What is FODMAP?

Understand. Eliminate. Reintroduce. Thrive.

The Low FODMAP diet is a 3-phase approach scientifically proven to help reduce IBS symptoms.

🍽️

1. Elimination

Remove high FODMAP foods for 2-6 weeks. Most people experience significant relief within 1-2 weeks.

↩️

2. Reintroduction

Systematically reintroduce FODMAP groups to identify your specific triggers. This takes 8-12 weeks.

👤

3. Personalisation

Build a long-term eating plan that works for your unique gut. Avoid only your identified triggers.

Why it works: FODMAPs are carbohydrates poorly absorbed by the small intestine. In the colon, gut bacteria ferment them, producing gas and drawing in water — causing bloating and digestive issues. Eliminating these foods gives your gut a chance to heal.

Foods to Avoid

Common high FODMAP foods

These foods may trigger symptoms in sensitive individuals.

🧅

Onion & Garlic

One of the largest sources of FODMAPs. Use garlic-infused oil instead.

🍎

Apples & Pears

High in fructose. Limit to specific low FODMAP fruits.

🥑

Avocados

High in polyols. Limit to ¼ of a fruit.

🍺

Wheat & Barley

High in fructans. Choose gluten-free alternatives.

🥛

Milk & Yogurt

High in lactose. Switch to lactose-free versions.

🍯

Honey & Syrup

Very high in excess fructose. Use maple syrup instead.

🫘

Beans & Lentils

High in GOS. Limit to small portions or avoid entirely.

🍫

Cashews & Pistachios

High in fructans. Choose macadamia nuts instead.

Tip: During elimination, focus on what you can enjoy. Once symptoms improve, work with a dietitian to reintroduce foods and discover your personal tolerance levels.
