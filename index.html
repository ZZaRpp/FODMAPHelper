What is FODMAP?

FODMAP stands for Fermentable Oligosaccharides, Disaccharides, Monosaccharides, and Polyols — carbohydrates poorly absorbed in the small intestine. They ferment in the colon, causing gas, bloating, and digestive discomfort in sensitive people.

The 3-Phase Protocol
1
Elimination — 2 to 6 weeks

Remove all high FODMAP foods. Most people see significant symptom relief within 2 weeks.

2
Reintroduction — 8 to 12 weeks

Reintroduce one FODMAP group at a time (3-day intervals) to identify your personal triggers.

3
Personalization — Ongoing

Build a long-term diet that avoids only your specific triggers, maximizing variety and nutrition.

Helpful Tips
●Portion sizes matter — even safe foods can trigger symptoms in large amounts
●Use garlic- and onion-infused oils for flavour (the FODMAPs don't transfer into oil)
●Keep this tracker to spot patterns between foods and symptoms
●Allow 3 days between reintroduced foods to observe reactions clearly
●Plain cooked meats, fish, and eggs are naturally FODMAP-free
●Hard aged cheeses (parmesan, cheddar) are low in lactose and safe
●Ripe bananas are high FODMAP; unripe (green-tipped) ones are safe
●Most symptoms improve within 1–2 weeks of the elimination phase
●Focus on the long list of foods you CAN eat, not on restrictions
●This diet is temporary — the goal is to find your threshold, not restrict forever

⚕️ Important: Always consult a registered dietitian experienced in the low FODMAP diet before starting. They ensure you follow it correctly and maintain proper nutrition throughout.
