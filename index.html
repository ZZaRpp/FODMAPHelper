What is FODMAP?

Understand. Eliminate. Reintroduce. Thrive.

The Low FODMAP diet is a 3-phase approach clinically proven to help reduce IBS symptoms like bloating, gas, pain and discomfort.

🥬

1. Elimination

Remove high FODMAP foods for 2-6 weeks to calm symptoms. Most people see relief within the first 2 weeks.

🔄

2. Reintroduction

Reintroduce FODMAP groups one at a time to identify triggers. Takes 8-12 weeks with 3-day intervals.

👤

3. Personalisation

Build a long-term diet that works for your unique gut. Maximum variety, minimum restriction.

Tips for Success

Simple habits that make a big difference

🍽️

Eat smaller meals

Large portions of even safe foods can trigger symptoms.

📸

Keep a food diary

Track meals and symptoms to identify your patterns.

🌿

Use infused oils

Garlic-infused oil adds flavour without FODMAPs.

💧

Stay hydrated

Drink plenty of water throughout the day.

⏱️

Space reintroductions

Wait 3 days between testing new foods.

😴

Get enough sleep

Stress and sleep affect digestive health.

FODMAP stands for Fermentable Oligosaccharides, Disaccharides, Monosaccharides, And Polyols. These are short-chain carbohydrates found in everyday foods that can trigger digestive symptoms in sensitive people.
