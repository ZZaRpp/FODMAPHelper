What is FODMAP?

FODMAP stands for Fermentable Oligosaccharides, Disaccharides, Monosaccharides, and Polyols. These are carbohydrates that are poorly absorbed in the small intestine and fermented by bacteria in the colon, causing gas, bloating, and digestive discomfort.

Diet Phases

Phase 1: Elimination (2-6 weeks)
Remove all high FODMAP foods to allow your digestive system to rest and symptoms to improve.
Phase 2: Reintroduction (8-12 weeks)
Gradually add back high FODMAP foods one at a time to identify which specific foods trigger your symptoms.
Phase 3: Personalization
Tailor your diet to include only the foods that don't trigger symptoms while maintaining nutritional balance.

Helpful Tips

• The low FODMAP diet is typically a 3-phase elimination diet to identify your specific food triggers
• Portion sizes matter - even low FODMAP foods can trigger symptoms in large amounts
• Keep a food and symptom diary to identify your personal triggers
• Use garlic and onion-infused oils instead of fresh garlic/onions for flavor
• Allow 3 days between reintroducing foods to observe reactions
• Work with a dietitian experienced in low FODMAP diet for best results
• Most symptoms improve within 1-2 weeks of eliminating high FODMAP foods
• Plain cooked meats, fish, and eggs are naturally FODMAP-free
• Focus on foods you CAN eat rather than restrictions
• This diet is temporary - meant to identify triggers, not for lifelong restriction

Important: Always consult with a healthcare provider or registered dietitian before starting this diet. They can ensure you're following it correctly and getting proper nutrition.
